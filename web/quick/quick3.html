Quickstart Guide: Classes, Race, and Power

This page last modified: August 26, 1998

Note that, with some exceptions, any race can try to be any class, but many combinations will not make good characters. Your race gives you a range of stats and you can only modify compared to a generic member of your race. Magic stat bonuses are no longer limited by racial maxes.

Hybrid classes such as deikhan and ranger have a number of overall improvements that improve their playability but also are more limited as casters now. Clerics as a whole were slightly downgraded by losing the pet skills (to be returned in the shaman class which will appear some time after the MUD goes into production), there have been some changes to fighting/engaged casting and mages were increased in power. Monks and thieves were restructured. Thieves have new trap skills and monks are going through a pretty thorough change in focus which may continue into 5.0.

Race and Class Combinations
Race Stat strengths Best classes
Human all average any
Elf dexterity, intelligence, focus, charisma, speed thief, ranger, monk, mage
Gnome intelligence, wisdom, focus, perception, charisma mage, cleric [and shaman]
Hobbit agility, dexterity, perception, karma, speed thief, monk
Ogre strength, brawn, constitution warrior
Dwarf strength, constitution, wisdom, karma warrior, deikhan, ranger, monk

Note: Demihuman races are generally weaker in the stats not mentioned as a stat strength than humans are.
