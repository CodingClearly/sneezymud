Claw/Killjoy/Leromir/Kendon/Darkwind

Real Name:  Kenny Walters
Residence: North Carolina
DOB: August 1, 1971
Marital Status: Single

I’ve been playing SneezyMUD since December 1998.  Blame Damian/Elon for introducing me to the MUD.  I was hooked the first time I played.  I’m a relative newbie compared to a lot of the players here, but most of them seem to have accepted me pretty well.  My current playing goal is to hit L50 at least once in every class.  When I first stated this goal, I was laughed at and told it would take me years to do it.  In a little over a year and a half, I’ve managed to hit L50 in 5 of the 7 available classes.  I’m going to provide some brief information on my L50’s.

Claw, L50 Ogre Warrior

Claw was my first character on Sneezy.  He had 37 glorious deaths before he finally made it to L50.  One of those occurred when Bryant typed ‘kill blob’ and attacked Claw instead of Nivek.  Oops.  He also was a victim of the infamous “Day of the Sandworm” in which he was swallowed twice by that particularly nasty mob.  Claw was the character with which I met many of the current players on the MUD.  He frequently grouped with Darsant, Cern, Bryant and Spineless.  He also is a former Guardian of the Step which is a position of import within the Brotherhood of Galek.  Claw occasionally comes out of the inn to socialize with other players or to even play.

Killjoy, L50 Elf Cleric

Killjoy was my first elf character and I have been hooked on that particular race since.  Killjoy is not the typical hang around and heal the tank cleric.  Killjoy has tanked several tough mobs due to the fact he is able to wear dwarven-sized armor.  Killjoy also was the character that first taught me the value of grouping to gain experience.  As Killjoy, I met Iradel who is one of my best friends on the MUD.    Killjoy was also there to help Iradel make it to L50 which brought me as much satisfaction as getting any of my on pc’s to that level.  Killjoy comes out of the inn occasionally when someone needs a little high level cleric help.

Leromir, L50 Elf Monk

Leromir may well be the best character I’ve made on Sneezy.  A lot of thanks goes to Drizzt who gave me advice on making an elf monk.  Leromir made the trip to L50 quickly.  Lero is approaching 50 crushed skulls in his career.   Lero also comes out of the inn on occasion when other high levels are on and need a monk hitter/tank.

Kendon, L50 Human Deikhan

Kendon, for a long time, was just a throw away character that was sitting in my pfile.  I never really played him seriously until Leromir hit L50.  Kendon is, like most deikhans, a strong tank.  He has completed all 3 deikhan sword quests and currently possesses his Holy Devastator and Deikhan’s Shield of Truth.  Kendon comes out of the inn usually to practice smite.  However, he is ready whenever a group wants a tank to take down a particularly tough mob.

Darkwind, L50 Elf Cleric/Thief

Darkwind is the character you’re most likely to see on the MUD at any given time.  He is currently a leader within the Order of The Serpents faction and is usually around in that capacity to help current Serpents and to recruit new ones.  Darky has spent a lot of time grouping with Enchantress aiding her in her quest to hit L50 which she succeeded in doing. Darkwind is also the character that I do the heaviest role-playing with.  As such, he has much more of a story than my other pc’s.  That story should eventually be available when The Order of the Serpents gets its on page on the Sneezy site.

 

Enchantress

Ever since I could remember, I was surrounded by a forest of trees and a family who loved me. We lived surprisingly in a mixed race village. A small community of hobbits, elves, humans, and we even had some civilized ogres living at our outskirts. From burrows to huts we seemed to get along peacefully. As I was growing up, I learned many things from my family and fellow citizens.

The ogres I actually got along with, and they use to throw me up on their shoulders and go crashing through the forest. They taught me strength and courage. (It's hard not to be brave when you have a humongous ogre spinning you around with one hand.) Sommerdai, who helped introduce me to these lands, was one of my greatest friends, I think I got my temper from her. The elves helped me to practice relaxation and meditation, when I was older we even dabbled a bit in the ways of magic. My family never forbid magical ways, just never promoted it. As such I have not done much in the ways of the mystical arts, though when I was young I could make a candle light by staring at it.

As I was growing up the hobbits became one of my biggest influences. Quick on speed and dexterity it was amazing what they could do. As kids we use to do sleight-of-hand magic tricks for the rest of the village for coins. As I grew older the hobbits taught me life long skills. How to find what I needed and pilfer what I couldn't afford. Their art of talk and persuasion was none like I've ever seen. They could make you believe just about anything if you listened to them long enough.

My family.....I loved my family very much. What I didn't learn from our villagers I learned from my family. I never took one minute for granted with them. They taught me independence, confidence, patience, and how to survive. My parents taught me trust, loyalty, and responsibility. They put their faith in me and taught me I could do anything I set my mind to. Above all else, they loved me and showed me that I was a strong, loving person who deserved to be treated as such.

Where did the name Enchantress come from? Well it certainly wasn't my real name. No I was born as....well, that name is known only to those who truly know me and whom I truly trust. However, it might have been the ogres who first called me Enchantress when they saw how flowers bloomed suddenly around me. Or was it the elves who first nicknamed me Enchantress when I first started practicing spells with them? Or it might have been the hobbits who saw how quickly I picked up their various arts of persuasion. In any case, I've been called Enchantress for as long as I can remember, so it's what I go by to this day.

When I turned eighteen, as was my right, I decided to leave our village and find a place of my own. I do go back to visit the village often, but this land is where I currently make my home.
