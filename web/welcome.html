Welcome to the World of SneezyMUD!

This page last modified: 09-26-97

Well my friend, it seems our Realm has grown, new magics have been unleashed, and the very scope of our adventures have been reshaped by the hands of the gods. I hope you'll continue your adventures, or begin a new one, here within the realms of SneezyMUD!
Here you will hopefully find adventure, enjoyment, and a welcome place in the taverns to tell your tales. The Immortals of the realm have placed themselves at your disposal to create a realm in which you can forget the toils of your borish world and droll life. Hopefully through these writtings you will gain the knowledge to survive the harsh adventures of the realm, the spark of interest to explore those far off regions or darkest holes, and a better knowledge and understanding of your fellow adventurers. So continue reading and let us start with the simple history of events leading to the SneezyMUD world you will see today.

I'm happy to welcome you to SneezyMUD on behalf of the implementors, gods, and devoted players. SneezyMUD began its history in May of 1992 from stock SillyMUD code acquired by Brutius. Since that time SneezyMUD has went through massive changes and three complete code upgrades brought about by thousands of man-hours put in by Brutius, his coding staff, and the Sneezy builders. SneezyMUD is now one of the top MUDs on the internet for its unique world and features. In the upgrade to version 4.0 SneezyMUD has been completely converted to C++ code and almost all traces of Diku or SillyMUD code are now gone.

SneezyMUD Invitation: You are hereby invited to try one of the most dynamic, fun, and complete fantasy muds on the internet. The dynamism comes from the attitude of the immortal staff. The fun from the spirit, attitude and general playfulness of our players. The depth of features and completeness has come from *constant* coding for 6+ years. Not 6 months from diku stock, or 2 years, but 6+ full years of mud coding from some of the most ambitious and dedicated mud coders on the internet.

SneezyMud's new version is SneezyMud 5.0. Those of you who tried Sneezy 2.5, 3.0, or 4.0 already know the advanced state of our code. We hope youu will be pleased by the advances since those code versions. Those of you who tried 4.0 will be also be surprised at the changes since 4.0. Although similar to 4.0, SneezyMud 5.0 has many more features and its balance has been shifted around a bit making it a slightly different and better mud. Those who have not tried Sneezy will be amazed at the code, world, and features.

Both code and world is fantasy based (the name Sneezy relates to our first machine and we have resisted changing it as a nod to our history). This fantasy world has the following features which you are invited to partake in:

SneezyMUD 5.X features:

General Features:

  • Account system (never forget a character name again)
  • Customizable terminal including color options
  • Customizable color system. Turn off areas of color.
  • Color substitution if you have problems with specific colors.
  • On-line aliases
  • On-line actions (limited)
  • On-line command history
  • Completely indexed and cross referenced documentation and auto-updating help files 500+ different commands all abbreviatable except quit :)
  • Ansi status bar or VT100 customizable prompt options
  • Full abbreviation of all objects, mobs, commands
  • On-line Player Usable Postoffice
  • On-line Player boards in most major cities
  • On-line Newspaper (the Herald)
  • Web Site
  • Listserver for balance discussions (its why you dont see us on usenet too much)
  • Regular immortal/mortal balance meetings
  • Immortals closely monitor bug, idea, and typo files
  • Mud wide quests
  • Stable Code Base: August 19 1998 (36 hours uptime no crash)
  • Percentage based equipment loads (No more reboot races)
  • On-line Building Creation (mobs world and objects)
  • Warrior, Mage, Cleric, Thief, Monk, Ranger, Deikhan classes
  • MultiClass capability (currently disabled)
  • Traditional but meaningful fantasy races
  • Customized stats based on race and class
    No more rolling/You pick your own stats
  • Mud philosophy based on tradeoffs
  • Balanced to be slightly hard but hopefully satisfying
  • No stock Code
  • No stock Zones
  • Implementation Team dedicated to responding to bugs, and not scared of implementing new features.

World Features:

  • All original 10000+ rooms and active building staff.
  • Mudwide hardcoded quests
  • Pk arena zone
  • Attachable tournament area for mud wide games
  • 10 exit directions, portals, grates, tunnels
  • Scan command for exploring
  • Room Height and limits (ogres might have to crawl through a hobbit dwelling)
  • 50+ meaningful terrain types governing all sorts of things
  • Weather affects
  • Analog lighting system which is affected by weather.
    • No rooms are simply lit or not lit. Lights and rooms have brightness ranging from full daylight to a moonless night.
  • Fully databased shop code (look at items in shop, find only items that fit you, look for specific pieces of equipment)
  • Underwater Zones requiring spells (underwater aspects affect a lot)
  • Rivers with current
  • Air rooms with gravity
  • Cliffs requiring climbing ability (fall and break bones)
  • Water rooms requiring swimming abilities
  • Door styles and types, door weights, door conditions (bash a door it stays bashed)
  • Ward doors and other magic doors
  • Numerous Spec-proc rooms

Mobile Features

  • 100+ meaningful npc races. (never get hit by a birds hands again) all npc's have stats, height, weights, characteristics, and skills based on their race.
  • All mobs remember, track, hunt but within the context of their race
  • Mobs can respond to questions and objects.
    • Quest giving mobs (for spells, skills and objects)
    • Manufacturing Mobs
    • Merchant mobs
    • Quests will remember where you are on the quest
  • Nocturnal and Diurnal mobs.
  • Mob skin types.
  • Mob anger, malice, suspicion, and greed (all affected by player action)
  • Mobs have pc skill base and have intelligent skill casting
  • Wimpy mobs, aggressive mobs, race hating mobs, grouping mobs, arrow shooting mobs, charmed mobs, teaching mobs, merchant mobs, swallowing mobs, looting mobs, mobs with tasks, rideable mobs, dragon mobs, guarding mobs, riding mobs, thief mobs, you name it.
  • Mount codes using height and weight of pc and mount.

    Object Features

    • 100+ meaningful material types
    • Armor condition and damage
    • Armor Repair (mobiles and pc's)
    • Item volume and weight
    • Weapon condition, sharpness, and bluntness
    • Weapon repair and sharpening (blunting)
    • Spell and skill affecting objects
    • Armor and Weapon monogramming and engraving at a cost
    • Racial sizing of *all* armor
    • Mages can scribe scrolls
    • Brewing of potions (coded but disabled till shaman come in game)
    • Traps and PoisonB
    • Proc objects
    • Equipment noise
    • Saddles, garrottes, containers, useable staffs, wands, potions, windows, portals, components, holy symbols, potions, scrolls, poisons, traps, spell components, holy symbols, moneypouchs and more.
    • Usable Furniture
      • Put your beer down on a table while not drinking it.
      • Sleep in a bed for better hp regeneration
    • Percentage loading and limited buff equipment so all pc's are different

    Combat Features

    • Meaningful limb based combat
    • Ranged combat
    • Mounted combat
    • Height based combat (affects all types of skills and combat results)
    • Weapon specializations
    • Original spell system
    • Original learning system. Learn by performing a skill.
    • Armor absorbs damage as well as provides ac
    • Limb damage and repair (doctors, bandages, splints, cleric spells)
      • Lose a limb, you cant hit but you might be able to limp to a town to find a doctor. Hand the limb to your friends.
    • Broken, bleeding, infected, missing, severed, diseased, leprose limbs
    • Beheadings and critical hits severing limbs
    • No saving throws but immunity based system (28 types)
    • Original Discipline learning system (100 training mobs spread through the world)
    • Mage casting with material, gestural, and oral components
    • Cleric Casting using holy symbols and piety.
    • Advanced Cleric piety system affected by the actions of players.
    • Clerics do not use mana.
    • Do away with quotation marks forever (one of the small things in life) Just cast spell target not cast "spell" target.
    • Critical success and failure in casting.
    • Ability to cast depends on the wizardry or faith of the player
    • Grouping limits
    • Afflictions

    Other

    • Commodity based economic system
    • Bank
    • Working Postoffice
    • Bounty and Equipment Hunting mobs (mobs can come after their equipment)
    • Quest Code that will remember what part of a quest you are on.
    • No good, evil, neutral alignments
    • Player enforced pk, ploot, and psteal
    • Helpful Immortal staff
    • Dedicated coders
